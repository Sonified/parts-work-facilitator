# Exercise 8: Accessing the Self Through Unblending
**Chapter:** 6 (Healing and Transformation)
**Phase:** Practicing Self-leadership with eyes open
**Time:** 10-15 minutes

## Purpose
Similar to the Path exercise, but bridges the gap between inner meditation and daily life -- you practice being Self-led with your eyes open.

## Full Exercise

Take a second to get comfortable and breathe deeply. Check in with the parts that you are actively getting to know and see how they're doing today. Remind them that you're there with them and that you can help them and that you care about them. You can also expand your purview to include other parts you might not know so well -- extend a recognition that you know they're there, that you care for them, and that you plan to keep getting to know them.

When you have the sense that your parts all feel at least recognized by you, ask them to relax and open up space in your mind and your body. Assure them that it will be just for a little while, and that the purpose is to let you and them know more of who you really are.

If they are willing, you'll notice the same experience of expanded and spacious awareness that you felt in the Path exercise.

This time, see if they'd be willing to let you hold this state -- Self-leadership -- even when you open your eyes. So if your eyes have been closed, just try opening them and see if you can feel the spaciousness. You might also find that when you open your eyes, parts jump back to attention to protect.

The practice of opening your eyes in that state is one step in a process that can lead you to experience a sense of being Self-led and embodied in your daily life.

By "practice," this does NOT mean Self-leadership is something you have to build up like a muscle. What we're doing instead is just helping parts increase their trust so that they allow you to embody and lead, so that they learn this is safe. The more they give it a try and see that nothing terrible happens, the more willing they'll be to keep trying. More and more you can experience this other way of being and extend it to your daily life.

At the end, remember to thank your parts for all they're doing and shift your focus back outside. See how much you can hold this sense of Self as you come back and go about your day.

## Key Insight
Self-leadership is not built up like a muscle. It's already there, fully formed. The "practice" is really about helping parts trust that it's safe to let Self lead. Compassion doesn't need to be developed -- it needs to be released.
