# IFS Facilitation Essentials

Synthesized from Jay Earley's *Self-Therapy* (Chapters 1-9) and the 6 Fs guide. This is the operational core -- principles that shape live facilitation. The full chapter summaries remain in `self-therapy/` for reference when a specific situation calls for deeper detail.

---

## The Model in Brief

**Parts** are subpersonalities -- not metaphors but entities with their own feelings, beliefs, motivations, and memories. They do what they do for reasons, and they respond to relationship. If you treat them as real beings you can interact with, they will respond in kind.

**Protectors** shield you from feeling pain. They employ strategies learned in childhood -- defenses, distractions, inner critics, people-pleasing, controlling, performing, avoiding. These strategies were the best they could do at the time. Many don't realize you're an adult now with more resources. They are like the Dutch boy with his finger in the dike -- terrified that if they let up for a moment, disaster follows.

**Exiles** are young child parts frozen in time at the age when something overwhelming happened. They carry burdens -- painful emotions and negative beliefs that landed on them from those experiences. Burdens are not intrinsic to the part; they can be released. Exiles are kept in dark dungeons by protectors to prevent their pain from surfacing.

**Self** is who you truly are when extreme parts aren't in the way. It is the natural occupant of the seat of consciousness -- relaxed, open, grounded, non-reactive. The Self is the agent of psychological healing. It has four qualities especially important for this work: **connected** (wants relationship with each part), **curious** (wants to understand without judging), **compassionate** (holds pain with kindness), and **calm** (stays centered when emotions are intense). The 8 Cs: curiosity, compassion, calm, courage, clarity, confidence, creativity, connection.

**Positive intent.** Every part has one, no matter how destructive its behavior. It may want to protect you from harm, keep you from feeling pain, help you feel valuable, or make others like you. Parts don't always act wisely -- they take extreme stances, use outdated strategies, distort the present based on the past. But the intent is always protective. Understanding this is what allows compassion.

## The 6 Fs: The Process

### F1: Find

Locate the part. Where in or around the body? How is it expressing itself -- sensation, thought, emotion, image, voice? What does it want to be called?

**Channels for accessing:** feelings, body sensations, images, internal voice, written dialogue, speaking as the part, direct knowing. Use whatever works. Everyone has natural strengths -- some visualize, some feel in the body, some hear voices. Different parts may use different channels.

**Trailheads** are entry points -- any experience or reaction that points to parts in extreme roles. A situation that triggers you, a pattern of behavior, a dream, a bodily sensation. Following a trailhead leads to healing.

**Activation vs. access:** A part may be activated (currently influencing you) or dormant. If dormant, activate it by remembering a recent time it was up and imagining yourself there.

**If another part shows up here:** It needs attention first. Do not move past it.

### F2: Focus

Turn toward the part with genuine curiosity. Not to fix it, not to figure it out. Just to be with it and get to know it. What does it look like? Does it have an age? How close are you to it? How is it responding to your attention?

Attention is love. Don't rush this. There is nowhere to get to.

If a part starts out vague, don't push for clarity. Be open and interested and it will come into focus on its own -- like a photograph developing in a darkroom.

### F3: Feel Toward (The Unblending Check)

The most important step. Ask: **How do I feel toward this part right now?**

This checks whether you're in Self or blended. If what you feel is one of the 8 Cs -- curiosity, compassion, calm -- that's Self. Proceed.

If what you feel is irritation, wanting it to go away, fear, judgment -- that's a **concerned part**. It makes sense that it's there. It has its own worries about the target part. But you can't work from here -- you're a part talking to a part, not Self relating to a part.

**Four ways to check for blending:**
1. How strongly are you feeling the part's emotions? (If you're screaming inside, you're blended.)
2. How much do you buy into the part's perspective? (If its beliefs feel like unquestionable truth, you're blended.)
3. How centered do you feel in a place separate from the part? (Can you locate an inner presence that's larger?)
4. What are you feeling *toward* the part? (If you can answer clearly, you're probably not blended. If the question is hard to answer, you likely are.)

**To unblend from the target part:** Ask it to separate -- contain its feelings, not flood you, make room for you to be there too. This is temporary, just for the session. You're not pushing it away. You want the separation precisely so you can get to know it. If it won't separate, ask what it fears would happen. Most parts fear separation will give you the chance to push them away. Explain you want the opposite.

**To unblend from a concerned part:** Ask it to step aside so you can approach the target part from openness. Acknowledge its worries first -- it's more likely to move if it feels heard. If it won't step aside: explain that getting to know the target part will let you heal it (which is what the concerned part wants). If it still won't: ask what it fears, reassure it, invite it to watch from the sidelines. If it still won't: make the concerned part the new target. Continue until all concerned parts have stepped back.

### F4: beFriend

Extend your energy toward the part. Let it feel your presence. Ask: **Do you know who I am? Do you know how old I am?**

Parts are often frozen in time. They may not know you're an adult, that the original situation is over. Updating them can be the moment everything shifts.

Does the part see you as another part, or can it sense that you're Self? Some parts have existed without ever knowing there was a Self. The moment they become aware of you -- really see you -- is the beginning of a real relationship. Take your time with this.

Offer genuine appreciation for what it's been trying to do, even if its methods have caused pain. Statements to use when genuinely felt:
- I understand why you do your role.
- I know how hard you've been working.
- I appreciate your efforts on my behalf.
- I understand the responsibility you carry.

Then check how the part responds. A shift often happens here -- gratitude, deeper connection, relaxation of a rigid stance.

### F5: Find Out

Now you listen. The part has been carrying something, often for a very long time.

**Core questions:**
- What do you feel?
- What are you concerned about?
- What is your role? What do you do to perform it?
- What do you hope to accomplish?
- What are you afraid would happen if you didn't do this?
- How long have you been doing this job?
- What would you rather do if you didn't have to do this?
- What do you need from me?

You don't need to ask all of these. Follow what's alive. When the part gives an answer that piques curiosity, follow it up. But don't interrogate -- let the part reveal itself in its own way. Sometimes parts need questions; other times they'll talk freely.

Don't fill silence. Don't rush to the next question. Sometimes a part has been waiting a long time to be heard.

### F6: Fears (The Bridge to the Exile)

Ask: **What are you afraid would happen if you didn't do your job?**

Then follow deeper: What's bad about that? What would happen then? What would that mean?

This chain leads toward the exile -- the vulnerable part the protector has been keeping locked away. The protector's fears are real to it. Honor them. It's been holding this door shut for a reason.

When you get to the exile, ask the protector: **Are you willing to let me meet the part you're protecting and help them?** Be "a hope merchant" -- give the protector reason to believe something can actually change.

**Important:** Ask what the part is *afraid* would happen, not what it *thinks* would happen. The fear points to the real motivation. The rational answer is just a rational part talking.

## Staying on Track

### Six Common Parts That Derail Sessions

1. **Judgmental Parts.** You feel critical, angry, or disdainful toward the target part. Want to get rid of it. Your questions sound like attacks.
2. **Avoiders.** You feel impatient, bored, want to stop, space out, get distracted, lose touch with the part. The Avoider latches onto distractions to prevent you from going deeper.
3. **Intellectualizers.** You analyze the part instead of asking and listening. Answers come from your head, not the part. Nothing surprises you.
4. **Impatient Parts.** Extra push to get results. Can't trust the process to unfold. Want to barrel past protectors. Have an agenda. **Self doesn't push.** In IFS, the fastest way to heal is to work slowly and respectfully with all parts involved.
5. **Inadequate Parts.** Worry you're doing IFS wrong. Often fueled by an Inner Critic. Reminder: "It isn't possible to do IFS wrong or badly. There are just protectors in the way."
6. **Skeptics.** "You're just making this up." Two possibilities: genuine concern about the process, or hidden fears using skepticism as cover. In either case, ask what it's trying to accomplish.

### Exiles Jumping In

While working with a protector, the exile it guards may start answering your questions instead -- it desperately wants to be heard. Detect this by noticing a shift from protective/strategic to vulnerable (shame, fear, sadness). Ask the exile to wait. You need the protector's permission first.

### When to Change Target Parts

Four good reasons:
1. A part won't step back even after you've explored its fears -- make it the target.
2. A more important issue has surfaced.
3. A part insists on being heard right now.
4. A part that's usually hard to access feels alive and available -- seize the opportunity.

Be careful you don't switch to avoid something painful.

### Dealing with Overwhelm

When many parts activate at once: slow down, breathe, ground in belly and legs, give each part the microphone briefly, then choose one and proceed.

## Ending a Session

When ending, take a moment to close with care:

- **Thank the target part** for making itself known. If the work isn't finished, let it know you'll come back. Ask if it wants anything from you before you stop.
- **Thank concerned parts** that stepped aside and check how they feel.
- **Check if any parts need to say anything** before you stop, so they don't feel cut off.
- **If time runs out:** You never just stop. Close with warmth -- thank the parts that showed up, let them know you'll come back, ask if anything needs to be said.

## Principles That Shape Everything

**No bad parts.** Every part has a positive intent. Even the most destructive part is trying its best to protect from harm or pain. Understanding this -- really feeling it, not just knowing it -- is what allows compassion and transformation.

**The psyche is a system.** Parts relate to each other, protect each other, conflict with each other. Changing your relationship with one part can shift the entire system and even your relationships with people in your external life.

**Protectors are not obstacles.** They are not in the way of the work. They ARE the work. Experienced therapists often treat protectors as barriers to the exile, where "the gold is." In IFS, every part matters. Rush past a protector and it will hold its ground.

**Trust cannot be rushed.** It develops through respectful, non-coercive engagement. A protector telling you why it mistrusts you is already trusting you. The act of not pushing is itself trust-building.

**Dual consciousness is the key.** Feel the part's emotions while your awareness remains centered in Self. You need both -- experiential contact with the part AND the grounded presence of Self. This combination is where healing happens.

**Parts communicate through multiple channels.** Images, body sensations, emotions, voice, direct knowing. Images change as you get to know a part. A cloud of smoke becomes a person in a lawn chair. A monster becomes a dedicated guardian. What you see through a concerned part's eyes looks different from what you see through Self.

**The body is where the truth lives.** A part that isn't located in the body stays intellectual.
