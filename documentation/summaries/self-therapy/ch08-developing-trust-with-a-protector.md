# Developing a Trusting Relationship with a Protector

Summary of Jay Earley, *Self-Therapy*, Chapter 8.

---

## Why Trust Matters

The entire IFS process depends on protectors eventually allowing you access to the exiles they guard. That won't happen unless a protector trusts you. And trust won't develop unless you are genuinely in Self -- not just tolerating the protector, not trying to get past it to the "real" work, but actually interested in connecting with it for its own sake.

Earley is direct about this: people who have experience working on themselves often treat protectors as obstacles, thinking the exile is "where the gold is." But in IFS, every part matters. None of them are just in the way. Each one is there for a reason and must be honored and respected. If you rush past a protector or give lip service to its positive intent, it will sense this and hold its ground.

---

## Appreciating Your Protectors

Most protectors have been working tirelessly for years, often doing a job they don't even enjoy. They believe their role is crucial -- that without them, you'd be flooded by pain or defenseless in a dangerous world. Earley compares them to the little Dutch boy with his finger in the dike: afraid that if they let up for even a moment, disaster would follow.

Now imagine doing that thankless, exhausting job for years, and the first time someone notices you, they say: "I wish you weren't ruining my life. Step aside so we can get to the important player." This is how protectors frequently experience us. They feel misunderstood and judged. What they long for is someone to understand why they do what they do and to appreciate the effort.

Even protectors that cause real problems in your life have their hearts in the right place. If you can't appreciate a protector for anything else, you can appreciate it for that.

Some protectors have mixed roles -- they do something genuinely useful but go overboard. Earley gives the example of his own "Accomplisher," a part that helped him be productive and successful but drained the aliveness out of daily life by treating every task as drudgery to push through. In a healthy internal system, such a protector would continue its useful role but without the extreme behavior driven by exile pain. The goal is not to abolish the protector but to work with it cooperatively.

---

## Earley's Own Story

Earley shares a turning point in his relationship with the Accomplisher. After years of trying to sidestep it, he realized he had never actually formed a trusting, cooperative relationship with it. He took time to reconnect, to appreciate not just what it was trying to protect him from but what it had genuinely contributed to his life. He shifted from trying to get rid of the part to wanting to collaborate with it.

The result was an unusual sense of well-being, an inner harmony among his parts. But then something unexpected happened: by late afternoon, the Accomplisher was feeling sad about all the times Earley had devalued it and tried to push it away. The part could finally feel its own pain because, for the first time, someone was treating it with respect instead of disdain.

This was an eye-opener: parts really do have feelings about how you treat them, and your relationship with them is crucial.

## Step P5: Developing a Trusting Relationship with a Protector

This is the final step in getting to know a protector. Check whether you genuinely understand and appreciate the protector's *efforts* on your behalf -- even if its *actions* cause problems. If you don't feel appreciation, one of two things may be happening:

1. You don't fully understand the protector's positive intent. Go back to the work described in Chapter 7.
2. You understand the intent but aren't in Self. Go back to Chapter 6 and unblend from any concerned parts.

Once you do feel understanding and appreciation, communicate this to the protector. Some statements Earley suggests, used only when they genuinely reflect how you feel:

- I understand why you perform your role.
- I understand why you think that is important. It makes sense to me.
- I know how hard you have had to work.
- I understand the responsibility you carry.
- I understand what you have sacrificed.
- I appreciate your efforts on my behalf.
- I appreciate what you did for me when I was a child.
- I appreciate what you have been trying to do for me throughout my life.
- I see how you contribute to my life.

After expressing this, check how the protector responds. You will often notice a shift -- a sense of gratitude, deeper connection, or some relaxation of the protector's rigid stance.

## Helping the Part to Be Aware of You

If you don't notice a response after expressing appreciation, the protector may not actually be aware of you. This sounds surprising -- you've been talking to it all this time -- but some parts are only aware of the past, not of you speaking to them in the present. They may not know you are an adult, or that you have a Self. All they know is the danger they see.

Parts are often narrower and more one-dimensional than a whole person. When triggered, they respond in stereotypical ways without much nuance. By engaging with them in IFS, you're helping them broaden their awareness.

How to check and foster awareness, depending on your channel of communication:

- **Image:** See if the part is looking at you. If not, ask it to look at you.
- **Emotion/body:** Temporarily switch places -- consciously identify with the part, enter its feeling, and from that place, look back to sense the presence of Self.
- **Internal dialogue:** Ask the part directly if it is aware of you. If not, ask it to notice you.

This moment -- when a part first becomes aware of your Self -- is the beginning of a real relationship. Many parts have existed without knowing there was a Self. It can be confusing for the part and for you. Take your time. Once your inner world reorganizes so that parts are aware of you, Earley says it often feels comforting and brings a sense of inner harmony. Your parts don't feel alone anymore.

Once the part is aware of you, say the statement of understanding or appreciation again. This time, it will likely respond clearly and strongly, because it can take in your attention and feel it.

## Transcript: Christine and the Confuser

Christine's Confuser makes her feel fuzzy, confused, and distracted -- it is terrified of something unspeakable happening. After she has gotten to know it, the part reveals that it feels overwhelmed by an impossible job but unable to stop. Then it asks directly for love, respect, and gratitude for suffering on her behalf.

Christine responds with deep appreciation -- so much that words can't capture it. And the Confuser begins to soften. Christine notices something important: the Confuser couldn't stop before because it had no connection with Self. There was nothing for it to release into, to relax into. Now it is softening because it senses that she is there.

This is a key insight: a protector that seems rigid and stuck may simply lack any awareness that Self exists. Once that connection forms, relaxation can begin. Christine also realizes that the Confuser's lifelong mistrust of love makes sense in a new way -- in order for it to trust love from anyone, it first had to trust her love for it.

## Transcript: Lisa and the Sooty Demon

Lisa's Sooty Demon is a fierce protector that wants to attack her sister to protect Lisa's heart. When Lisa moves from fear to admiration -- seeing it as incredibly dedicated rather than dangerous -- the part responds with surprise and delight. It shuffles its feet, says "Oh gosh," then excitedly shows off what it can do.

The shift ripples through Lisa's entire internal system. The Watcher relaxes, the Little Girl giggles, the Mothering Part feels her load lighten. And, strikingly, Lisa's sister no longer seems paired with the Sooty Demon -- she seems fresh, as if Lisa is encountering her for the first time.

Earley draws a broad lesson: having a good relationship with each of your parts can change your entire relationship with yourself and with others. Parts are a system, and when you change your relationship with one, it can shift how you feel about all of them.

## When a Part Doesn't Relate to You

Sometimes a protector actively mistrusts you -- it won't talk, won't show itself, turns its back, or disappears mid-session. First, check whether a concerned part is blocking your access. Another protector may think the target part is too dangerous to connect with, acting like a censor. Call that part forward and work with it as you would any concerned part (Chapter 6).

---

## When a Part Doesn't Trust You

If no concerned part is blocking access and the protector still won't engage, the issue is trust. Ask the protector whether it trusts you -- even a part that won't answer other questions will often answer this one.

Two common reasons for mistrust:

### 1. You're blended with a concerned part without realizing it

Parts are very sensitive to your attitudes. They can tell if you are coming from a pure place or if a subtle judgment is coloring your approach. Any hint of judgment makes it unsafe for them to open up. Check for any subtle angry or judgmental parts. If you find one, work with it as a concerned part until it steps aside.

### 2. The protector has a history of betrayal

The part has been hurt, abandoned, or betrayed in the past, and it has decided to never trust anyone again -- including you. It may not even be aware that there is a Self. Your job is to work with it sensitively and respectfully until trust begins to form.

Some practical guidance from Earley:

- **Be patient.** If the part won't say anything beyond "I don't trust you," just be with it without prodding. Let go of expectations. Earley compares this to sitting quietly with a wild deer -- you wouldn't rush it. Just offer your attention without hoping for anything in return.
- **Ask what it doesn't trust.** Trust is broad. There is usually something specific: it may not trust that you care, that you're strong enough, that you won't abandon or attack it, or that you won't dismiss it.
- **Invite the part to tell its story.** Even if you think you know, ask in its own words. You may be surprised. Take time to empathize, as you would with a friend.
- **Don't expect instant trust.** When the part says "You weren't there for me back then," apologize and explain that you were young too. Let it know you're older now, wiser, and present.
- **Don't pressure it.** Trust comes on its own time frame. Being non-coercive is itself trust-building. The very act of not pushing goes a long way.

And a crucial reframe: if a mistrustful part is telling you why it mistrusts you, it is already trusting you. That's probably the most important thing it could share about itself. There is no need to get it to declare trust -- it has been building trust through the conversation. Continue respectfully, and eventually the protector will allow you to work with the exile it guards. Once the exile is healed, the protector will likely trust you completely.

## Transcript: Carl and the Angry Part

Carl's Angry Part won't speak to him. Its image has its back turned, glaring. First, Earley helps Carl check whether he's truly in Self -- Carl discovers a Judgmental/Annoyed Part, which steps aside. But the Angry Part still won't engage. Asked directly, "Do you trust me?" -- it answers, "Hell, no!"

Earley coaches Carl to just hang out with the part, not pushing. An Impatient Part surfaces and steps aside. Carl sits quietly, offering his presence without agenda. Gradually, the image sharpens. The part slowly turns around, growing curious about who Carl is.

Eventually the part reveals it doesn't trust Carl to mean what he says -- safe-seeming people have turned on him before. (Carl's father was an alcoholic, kind when sober, abusive when drunk.) The part says, "You weren't there when I needed you. Why should I believe you'll be there now?"

Carl apologizes, explains he was just a kid then, and promises to be there now. The part doesn't fully trust this -- and Earley says that's completely fine. In fact, the part already trusts Carl to a significant extent, or it wouldn't be revealing so much. The act of not pushing for more trust is itself what builds it. The part softens, its stance more relaxed, its scowl gone.

## Helping a Protector Relax in Real Time

Once you've gotten to know a protector and built trust, you can work with it when it gets activated in daily life:

1. **Notice the activation.** A protector blends with you because a life situation has triggered it.
2. **Unblend.** Ask the part to separate from you so you can be in Self. Remind it that you are here, so it doesn't have to handle this alone. You are an adult with more capacity than the part realizes (it may still think you are a child).
3. **Understand the fear.** Ask what it's afraid of and what its role is.
4. **Ask it to relax and let you lead.** This won't fully resolve the fear -- that requires healing the exile underneath -- but the protector may relax enough to let you act from Self.
5. **Ask what it needs.** The part may need reassurance that you have the skills to handle the situation. If you don't, do some preparation first.
6. **Follow through.** If things go well when Self leads, the part will trust you more in the future.

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Developing a Trusting Relationship with a Protector

Do a session using steps P1-P5, paying particular attention to Step P5. Follow the Help Sheet to keep track. When finished, write down:

- Name of the part
- What it feels
- What it looks like
- Where it is located in your body
- What it says
- How it makes you behave
- What situations activate it
- What concerned parts react to it
- What its positive intent is
- What it is protecting you from
- What you understand or appreciate about the part
- How the part responded to your saying that

### Exercise 2: Gaining a Part's Trust

Choose a protector you've tried to work with that hasn't been responsive. Note how it hasn't responded, then:

1. Make sure you are in Self -- check for and unblend from any concerned parts
2. See if the protector will now talk to you
3. If it still won't, work with it as described in this chapter
4. Explore: What does the part not trust about you? What happened in the past to make it mistrustful?

This may take more than one session.

### Exercise 3: Helping a Protector to Relax in Real Time

Think of a situation where a protector gets triggered and causes problematic behavior. Do a session to get to know it and form a trusting relationship. Then:

1. Ask the protector if it will let you lead the next time the situation arises (make sure you have the life skills to handle the positive behavior you're aiming for)
2. If it says no, ask why, and work with that
3. Set an intention to notice when the protector activates in daily life
4. When it does, work with it in the moment: ask it to relax and let you lead from Self
5. Track the results -- take notes as soon as you can after each instance
6. Review regularly (daily or weekly, depending on how often the situation arises)
7. After successful episodes, check whether the protector now trusts you more

## Chapter Summary

This chapter covers Step P5 -- the final step in getting to know a protector. The core ideas:

- Protectors need genuine understanding and appreciation, not lip service.
- Trust cannot be rushed. It develops through respectful, non-coercive engagement.
- Some protectors are deeply mistrustful due to a history of betrayal. Patience and presence are required -- sit with them the way you would sit with a frightened animal.
- A protector that is telling you why it mistrusts you is already trusting you.
- Changing your relationship with one part can shift your relationship with your entire internal system and with the people in your life.
- Once trust is established, the protector will eventually allow access to the exile it guards. Healing the exile completes the circle of trust.
