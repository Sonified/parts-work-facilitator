# TLPCA IFS Introduction - Summary

This is a clinical training presentation (adapted from the IFS Level 1 Training Manual). It's more practitioner-oriented than the worksheet, but contains the most concise breakdown of parts roles and the 6 Fs unblending protocol.

## 6 Basic Assumptions of IFS

1. The mind naturally contains many sub-personalities or parts
2. Everyone has Self, and Self can and should lead the internal system
3. Parts develop burdens and get forced into roles they don't like due to trauma and attachment injuries
4. Every part has a positive, non-extreme intention. No "bad" parts -- therapy aims to help parts find their non-extreme roles
5. Parts form a complex system (polarizations, alliances, hierarchies). When the system reorganizes, parts can change rapidly
6. Changes in the internal system affect the external system and vice versa -- both levels need to be assessed

## The Three Roles of Parts

### Managers (Proactive Protectors)
- **Motto:** "Never again!"
- **Job:** Keep the person in control of every situation and relationship to prevent activation of exile pain
- **Common traits:** Controlling, striving, perfectionism, judging/criticizing/evaluating, risk avoidance, caretaking, people-pleasing, worrying, denying problems, passive pessimism, avoiding, numbing, rejecting parts/others/shame
- **They carry:** Responsibility, guilt, anger, worry, anxiety, fatigue
- **Key quality:** They feel tired and old

### Firefighters (Reactive Protectors)
- **Job:** Activate when vulnerability is triggered; do whatever it takes to eliminate pain, regardless of cost
- **Common traits:** Impulsive, self-absorbed, distracting, dissociating, stifling, soothing, disinhibited, shut-down, humor to avoid, defensive/aggressive, self-harming, rebellious
- **Behaviors:** Addictions (sleep, shopping, sex, diet, exercise, video games), binge eating, suicidality, self-harm, violence, dissociation, distraction, obsession, compulsion, fantasy, rage
- **They carry:** Stress, anger, shame, guilt, fatigue
- IFS sees these as protectors -- no bad parts

### Exiles (Protected Parts)
- **Experience:** Rejected, made worthless, ashamed, confused
- **They long to:** Come out of hiding to be healed, valued, loved
- **When banished:** Their curiosity, spontaneity, courage, connectedness get banished too
- **They carry:** Deep shame, fear, helplessness, despair, worthlessness, trauma memories, sorrow, fragility, emptiness
- **Key tension:** They want redemption, which makes protectors see them as threats
- **Common traits:** Dependent, hopeless, worthless, powerless, angry, open, abandoned, fearful, unlovable, shamed, fragile, sensitive, sad, empty, hurt, frozen

## How Parts Show Up
Thoughts, feelings, sensations, memories, inner voices, words, images

## The 6 Fs: Unblending Protocol

This is the step-by-step process for getting to know and separate from a part:

1. **Find** -- Locate the part in or around your body. How does it show up? How is it expressing itself (thought, image, sensation, memory, emotion)? What does it want to be called?

2. **Focus** -- Put attention on it. What do you notice? Shape, color, age? How is it responding to your attention? Look at it with curiosity. Let it know you intend to get to know it better.

3. **Feel** -- How do you feel toward the part? (This is where you assess Self energy.) If you don't feel one of the 8 Cs (curiosity, compassion, calm, etc.), another part is present -- acknowledge it and ask it to step aside. How open is your heart toward the part?

4. **beFriend** -- Establish relationship between Self and part. Extend your energy to it. Ask if it knows who you are, how old you are. Update it if needed. Does it see you as another part? Offer appreciation for its positive intention.

5. **Find Out** -- What does it want you to know? What is its intention for you? What is its job? What would it rather do? What does it need?

6. **Fears** -- What is it afraid would happen if it didn't do its job? What's bad about that? What would happen then? Are you willing to let me meet the part you protect? Give the part hope ("being a hope merchant"). Does it protect parts stuck in past chaotic times?

## For the Therapist/Practitioner

- Come to the session in Self energy, embodying the 5 Ps: Presence, Patience, Perspective, Persistence, Playfulness
- Resistance = Protection (not defiance)
- It takes time for clients to access parts or unblend
- Check for fears and concerns the system has about the target part
- Get permission from protectors before moving forward
- When starting, parts tend to appear like "popcorn" until they trust the process

## Two Parts of the IFS Model
- **Part I: Unblending** (the 6 Fs above -- what you can practice on your own)
- **Part II: Unburdening** (deeper work; the IFS Institute recommends Level 1 training before engaging in this)
