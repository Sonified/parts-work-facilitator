# Exercise 11: Advanced Parts Mapping
**Chapter:** 9 (Life Lessons and Tor-Mentors)
**Phase:** Using triggers to map deeper subsystems
**Time:** 15-20 minutes

## Purpose
Use a triggering situation to map a full "clove of garlic" -- the protectors, their relationships, and what they protect. This is the advanced version of Exercise 2.

## Caution
You'll be learning about exiles your protectors guard. You won't go to those exiles directly, but even learning about them can be triggering. If at any point it feels like too much, pause, step out, check in with yourself, and remind your parts that you're still there.

## Full Exercise

Think of a time when you were quite triggered by something. As you think of that situation, notice the triggered parts and then pick a protector from that clove to focus on. Place your attention on that protector exclusively, finding it in your body or around your body, and notice how you feel toward it.

If you feel anything extreme toward it -- you're afraid of it, for example -- that's just another part. Move your focus to that one for a second.

Notice the two of them -- the original protector and the one who has attitude about it -- and how they fight inside of you. Notice if there are any other protectors that jump in to ally with either side or take a third position.

So far, we're not interacting with any of these parts -- we're just getting a sense of this network that comes up around this trigger. We're getting to know the protectors involved.

At some point, as you watch this dance of your protectors, see if you can open your mind more toward them so you can learn about them. If you can't get there, that's okay -- just spend the exercise noticing.

If you do become interested in what all of this activity is about, go ahead and ask each one about the vulnerability it protects. What does it fear would happen if it didn't take its position?

If your protectors answer that question, you will begin learning about the exiles that drive these extreme responses. Without going directly to those exiles, see how much of a sense of them you can get. Can you guess what they're like? Can you become more aware of their vulnerability?

As you learn more about what your protectors are taking care of, it may help you open your heart more to them, because you get more of a sense of what they're dealing with and how high the stakes are. Often, these protectors are like parents who have a highly vulnerable child. They fight and polarize about how best to protect that child, because the stakes are so high. The difference is that these protectors aren't old enough to be parents -- usually they're young themselves, in over their heads, just trying to do their best.

Let them know you get all that. Tell them you'll keep working with them. Let the exiles know you're aware that they're in there -- you can't visit with them today, but at some point, you're going to try to help them too.

Now bring your focus back outside. You're leaving your internal world, but you're not forgetting it.

## Key Concept: Tor-Mentors
Difficult people and events that trigger you are mentoring you about what needs to heal. The emotions they trigger are trailheads to key exiles. Not every difficult situation is a lesson, and staying in abusive relationships "to learn" is never the point. But when parts do get triggered, it never hurts to pay attention and take care of them.
