# Exploring Your Inner Parts: IFS Worksheet - Summary

This is a comprehensive self-guided worksheet for identifying, understanding, and beginning to relate to your parts. It's the most detailed single document in the library -- part explainer, part workbook.

## Core Framework

**The 5 types of parts:**

1. **Inner Child** -- younger versions of you (baby through young adult, ~21). They hold both happy and painful unresolved memories. There may be several at different ages. When you're ready to process old material, emotions and memories surface.

2. **Exiles** -- parts carrying emotional pain, traumatic memories, vulnerable emotions. Typically younger parts that experienced pain or unmet needs. Pushed out of awareness to protect you from re-experiencing their pain.

3. **Managers** -- proactive protectors. They prevent exiles from being triggered and maintain control/safety. Show up as perfectionism, self-criticism, rigidity, overachievement. They developed as coping strategies.

4. **Firefighters** -- reactive protectors. They emerge when exiles get activated and overwhelm you. They distract or numb through impulsive behaviors (substance use, binge eating, self-harm, compulsions). Temporary relief, long-term cost.

5. **The Self** -- your true essence. Characterized by curiosity, compassion, wisdom, calmness. Center of stability. Provides guidance, healing, leadership. The goal is for Self to lead, relating to all parts from understanding and compassion.

## The 8 Cs of Self

These are how you know you're in Self (vs. blended with a part):

- **Curiosity** -- non-judgmental, open exploration of your parts
- **Compassion** -- warmth and kindness toward all parts regardless of their behavior
- **Calmness** -- inner peace and centeredness; emotional regulation vs. overwhelm
- **Courage** -- willingness to face uncomfortable parts and emotions
- **Clarity** -- seeing the bigger picture and interconnections between parts
- **Confidence** -- trusting your own inner wisdom to navigate this work
- **Creativity** -- imaginative, intuitive, flexible approaches to healing
- **Connection** -- deep authentic relationship between you and your parts; trust and collaboration

## Signs of Inner Conflict (How You Know Parts Need Attention)

- **Internalized aggression** -- severe self-criticism, lashing out at yourself, anger turned inward (often from not having permission to be angry as a child)
- **Shame** -- distorted identity, feeling damaged/unworthy (from being ignored, neglected, shamed as a child); shows up in relationships, work, parenting, boundaries
- **Regression** -- feeling like a child in an adult body, tantrum-like reactions, power struggles that echo family dynamics. Ask: "How old does this part feel?"
- **Perfectionism** -- pressure for everything to be perfect, suffering consequences of constant striving
- **Contradictory thoughts/feelings** -- pulled in different directions (risk vs. safety, action vs. avoidance)
- **Inner critic/saboteur** -- constant undermining voice
- **Procrastination** -- resistance or fear of action
- **Feeling stuck/indecisive** -- internal tug-of-war blocking decisions

## Practical Exercises in This Document

1. **Identify and profile two parts** -- name them, describe their role, triggers, how they interact, their positive intentions, related memories/traumas, and what they need to feel heard
2. **Empty chair dialogue for internalized anger** -- invite the "emotional bully" part to a conversation, reassure it, challenge beliefs, discover who you're actually angry at (often someone who failed to protect you, or the world/society itself)
3. **Shame shifting** -- catch shame-filled thoughts and replace them; notice body language (leaning back, lowering voice, hesitating) and practice leaning in, speaking up, standing tall
4. **Regression drawing** -- draw your adult self gently holding or sitting with the younger activated part, then write what you'd say to them
5. **Intentional imperfection** -- deliberately do one thing imperfectly and notice what happens
6. **Integration reflection** -- describe your core Self qualities, imagine parts working together harmoniously, identify what resources from Self you can bring to support the whole system

## Key Insight for Practice

The goal is not to suppress or battle parts, but to build trust and understanding. Recognize and honor the positive intentions of each part. Help them feel seen and heard. Create an internal environment where parts feel safe, supported, and aligned with your values.
