# Exercise 6: Daily IFS Meditation
**Chapter:** 4 (More on Systems)
**Phase:** Ongoing daily practice
**Time:** 15-20 minutes

## Purpose
A daily practice for building ongoing relationships with parts, helping them trust Self, and gradually increasing embodiment. This is the recommended daily meditation for anyone on the IFS path.

## Full Exercise

Take a second to get comfortable. If it helps to take some deep breaths, go ahead. Focus on the parts you're getting to know. The goal is to follow up and see how they're doing now and if there's anything they need, if there's more they want you to know. This is all in service of building an ongoing relationship with your parts so they feel more connected to you, less isolated and alone.

At some point, remind them that you're there with them, that you care about them, and tell them a little more about who you are, because even as you work with parts, they often forget these things until they've been unburdened. It never hurts to remind them that they're not alone anymore and that you're not a young child anymore and that you can care for them in a way they need.

The goal is to take your parts as seriously as you take your literal children, if you have children. The good news is that your parts don't need nearly as much maintenance or nurturance as literal children do -- they often just need to know about this connection you're building, just to be reminded of it.

Then at some point you can expand your purview and invite any other parts that need attention to come to you. On different days, different parts will show up. Just get to know them and what they need from you, and also let them know who you are and that they're not alone anymore.

**Optional piece:** If you'd like to, revisit each of these parts, inviting them to relax inside and open space just for a few minutes. Ask them to trust that it's safe to let you more into your body. Their energy tends to make it harder for you to be embodied when they're triggered. If they're willing, you'll notice a shift each time they relax -- you'll feel more space inside your mind and body.

Remind them that it's just for a few minutes, just an experiment. They don't have to if they don't want to. But if they are willing, notice the qualities of this increase in spaciousness and embodiment. Notice what it feels like to be more in your body with a lot of space.

You might notice a shift in your breathing or your ability to be present. You might feel your muscles relax and a sense of well-being. You might also notice a kind of energy running through your body, making your extremities tingle.

If your parts are having a lot of trouble relaxing, it just means they need more attention at some point. Let them know you get it, that there's no pressure.

When the time feels right, begin to shift your focus back out, thank your parts for whatever they let you know, and remind them you're going to do this practice more in the future.

## As a Life Practice
This doesn't have to be limited to 20-minute sessions. Throughout your day, pause and notice how much Self is present:
- How open is your heart?
- Is your mind open or do you have a strong agenda / pressured thoughts?
- What's the resonance of your voice?
- Is Self energy flowing?
- Is there tension in your forehead or weight on your shoulders (common manager locations)?

Find your own markers. When you notice departures from Self, find the activated part and remind it that it's safe to trust you.
