# The 6 Fs: A Guide to Unblending

The 6 Fs are the structured process at the heart of IFS practice. They take you from being blended with a part -- where its feelings feel like your feelings, where it feels like "me" -- to being in relationship with it from Self. Each step builds on the one before. You don't skip ahead. If you get stuck at one step, that's information -- usually another part has shown up.

This is Part I of the IFS model (Unblending). Part II (Unburdening) is the deeper healing work that the IFS Institute recommends Level 1 training for. But the 6 Fs on their own are powerful -- a part that feels seen and heard by Self can shift significantly just through this process.

---

## F1: Find

Start by noticing what's activated. Something is happening in your body -- a tightness, a heaviness, a heat, a contraction. Or maybe it's showing up as a thought that won't stop, an image, a voice, a memory. That's a part. Your job right now is simply to locate it.

Where do you notice it in or around your body? How is it expressing itself -- as a sensation, a thought, an emotion, an image? What does it want to be called? (If it doesn't have a name yet, a working label is fine. The real name comes from the part when it's ready.)

Check with other parts before proceeding: are there any fears or concerns about getting to know this part? If another part jumps in with "don't go there" or "this is scary," that part needs to be heard first.

---

## F2: Focus

Now turn your attention toward it. Not to fix it. Not to figure it out. Just to be with it and get to know it.

What do you notice about it? Does it have a shape, a color, a texture? Does it feel like it has an age? How close are you to it? How is the part responding to you putting your attention on it -- does it pull back, open up, get louder?

Let it know your intention is simply to get to know it better. Look at it with curiosity. You're not trying to change anything yet.

---

## F3: Feel Toward (The Unblending Check)

This is the most important step. Ask yourself: how do I feel toward this part right now?

This is where you find out whether you're in Self or still blended. If what you feel toward the part is one of the 8 Cs -- curiosity, compassion, calm, courage, clarity, confidence, creativity, connection -- that's Self energy. You can proceed.

If what you feel is something else -- irritation, wanting it to go away, fear of it, judgment, "I shouldn't be feeling this" -- that's not Self. That's another part showing up. It makes sense that it's there. Acknowledge it warmly ("I hear you, that makes sense") and ask if it can give you a little space so you can be with the target part. If it can't step back, that's ok -- you may need to work with that part first, or it may just need to be heard for a moment before it's willing to give space.

The question to hold: how open is your heart toward this part?

---

## F4: beFriend

Once you can feel some openness, warmth, or curiosity toward the part, you're ready to be in relationship with it. This is where something starts to shift -- not through technique, but through the part feeling seen by Self.

Extend your energy toward it. Let it feel your presence. Ask it: do you know who I am? Do you know how old I am?

Many parts are frozen in time. They think you're still a child, still in the situation where they first took on their role. If so, update the part -- let it know you're an adult now, that the original situation is over. This can be a powerful moment. In the TJ session from No Bad Parts, when TJ's fear part learned she wasn't young anymore, it was "shocked" and settled.

Does the part see you as another part, or can it sense that you're Self? Offer appreciation for what it's been trying to do for you. Even if its methods have been painful, its intention has been protective. It took on this job for a reason.

---

## F5: Find Out

Now you listen. The part has been carrying something, doing a job, often for a very long time. This is where you hear its story.

Questions to ask:

- What does it want you to know?
- What is its job? How long has it been doing it?
- How did it get this job?
- What is it trying to do for you?
- What would it rather do if it didn't have to do this job?
- What does it need from you?
- Is it tired?
- Is there anything else it wants you to know?

Let it speak. Don't rush to the next question. Sometimes a part has been waiting a long time to be heard and it just needs space to say what it's been holding.

---

## F6: Fears (The Bridge to the Exile)

This is where you learn what the protector is guarding. Ask: what are you afraid would happen if you didn't do your job?

Then follow the thread deeper: what's bad about that? And what would happen then? And what would that mean for you?

This chain of questions leads toward the exile -- the vulnerable part that the protector has been keeping locked away. The protector's fears are real to it. Honor them. It's been holding this door shut for a reason.

When you get to the exile, ask the protector: are you willing to let me meet the part you're protecting and help them? Schwartz calls this step "being a hope merchant" -- giving the protector reason to believe that something can actually change if it lets you through.

The protector may also reveal that the exile is stuck in a specific time -- "stuck in times that were chaotic" or "when things were falling apart." This is important information for the deeper work.

---

## What to Watch For (from the training doc)

**Parts appearing like popcorn.** "When first starting parts tend to appear like popcorn until they trust the process." Pick one and stay with it.

**Resistance is protection.** "Resistance = Protection." If something feels stuck or blocked, that's a protector doing its job. Work with the resistance, not against it.

---

## The 8 Cs (Self Energy)

These are how you know you're in Self rather than blended with a part:

**Curiosity** -- genuine interest in the part, wanting to understand it
**Compassion** -- warmth and kindness toward the part
**Calm** -- inner steadiness, not reactive
**Courage** -- willingness to be with what's difficult
**Clarity** -- seeing the bigger picture, recognizing what's a part and what's Self
**Confidence** -- trusting your capacity to be with this
**Creativity** -- openness to what might emerge
**Connection** -- feeling in relationship with the part, not separate from it

If you're not feeling any of these toward the target part, another part is present. That's F3.

---

## Notes From Practice

**The flood.** Sometimes, when a protector starts to relax, multiple exiles rush the door at once -- too much to sit with all at the same time. What helps: telling each one "I see you. I'm not going to forget you. I need to come to you one at a time." The protector was right to be cautious; it knew the full inventory.

**Fatigue as cost of protection.** Significant fatigue can lift when a protector holding a door shut is recognized and relaxes its grip. The fatigue wasn't genuine tiredness -- it was the energetic cost of the protector's effort.

**Acknowledge before asking a part to step back.** During F3, when a non-target part shows up and is asked to step back without being acknowledged first, it can trigger more parts laterally and derail the process. A quick warm acknowledgment ("that makes sense, I hear you") before the ask keeps things moving.
