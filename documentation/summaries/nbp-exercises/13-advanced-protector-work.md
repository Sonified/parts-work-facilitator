# Exercise 13: Advanced Protector Work (Legacy Burdens)
**Chapter:** 10 (The Laws of Inner Physics)
**Phase:** Working with parts you're most ashamed of
**Time:** 15-20 minutes

## Purpose
Approach the parts you most want to deny -- parts carrying legacy burdens like racism, sexism, or other beliefs you find shameful. The point: going to war against these parts just strengthens them. Awareness and compassion are how they change.

## Full Exercise

Take a second to get comfortable. Start by touching base with the parts you've already been working with. See how they're doing and remind them that you're there with them.

Now: you can't grow up in the US or other countries with a long history of racism and not carry that legacy burden. No matter what your race, no matter how much anti-racism work you've done, it's still likely that there's a part carrying that burden.

That's the part to look at -- the racist. The one who harbors white supremacist beliefs and says nasty things in your head at times. Even those who honestly aren't aware of their own racism at first will find it if they are patient.

You don't need to get close to that racist part. Just notice how you feel toward it. When another part speaks up -- particularly one that tells you to be ashamed or afraid of the racist part -- let that protector know that letting you get closer to the racist part will actually help it change, and tell it that the exiling approach doesn't really work.

For now, it may be enough to acknowledge the racist part's existence and commit to doing more work with someone who can help.

Reminders from the IFS perspective:
- That inner racist is just a part of you. Much of you isn't that way.
- It's not some unworkable bundle of racism. Like all your other protectors, this part can unburden and transform too.
- There's no shame in having this part. Racism is a legacy burden that's pervasive in this culture.
- This legacy burden often permeates many parts, so don't be disappointed if it doesn't totally disappear after unburdening one.

Eventually, you may find that this racist part is a protector and you need to heal the exile it protects before it will unburden. Or it might simply be carrying the cultural legacy burden and will be more than willing to unload it when you let it know that's possible.

When you feel you've reached a stopping place, thank your parts, and come back to the outside world.

## Broader Application
Use this same process with other parts you're ashamed of or fear:
- The one that gives you embarrassing sexual fantasies
- The one that secretly delights when friends fail
- The one who holds beliefs you find unacceptable

These are all young, misguided inner children. Like misguided external children, they deserve guidance and love, not scorn, shame, and abandonment.

## Key Insight
Until you can unburden these parts, it's far better to just be aware of them. Actively remind them, compassionately, that what they say and think isn't right. Going to war against a part usually just strengthens it. Exiling it and pretending it's not there makes it much harder to unburden and counter the potential harm.
