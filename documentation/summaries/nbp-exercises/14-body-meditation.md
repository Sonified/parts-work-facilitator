# Exercise 14: Body Meditation
**Chapter:** 11 (Embodiment)
**Phase:** Working with physical symptoms and body tension
**Time:** 10-15 minutes

## Purpose
Explore whether physical symptoms, tension, or pain might be parts trying to communicate with you through your body. This is NOT about blaming yourself for symptoms -- it's about listening.

## Full Exercise

Focus on your body. If you have a medical condition, feel free to focus on its manifestation. If you don't, find a place in your body that doesn't quite feel like you -- any spot of tension, pressure, congestion, achiness, or fatigue. We're looking for a sensate trailhead -- a point to focus on and begin exploring.

When you have found one, place your attention on it and notice how you're feeling toward it. You may feel frustrated or defeated by it or wish you could get rid of it, all of which are understandable. But ask those parts to give you a little space so you can just get to know it.

If you can shift into that curious place, ask what it wants you to know.

Wait for an answer. All of your thinking parts who want to speculate can relax. If no answer comes, that's fine -- it could be just a physical issue that's not related to your parts at all.

If you do get an answer, stay with the sensation as if it were a part and ask the kinds of questions we ask parts:

- "What are you afraid would happen if you didn't do this to my body?"
- "Why do you feel like you have to use my body?" (Why can't it talk to you directly?)
- "What do you need from me to not have to do this to my body?"

When the time feels right, thank the part for whatever it shared and begin to shift your focus back outside.

## Context from the Book

Schwartz's experience across decades of practice:
- Parts can deliberately target different organs or systems when they can't get through to you directly
- They know about your genetic predispositions and can push physical buttons
- A published study on rheumatoid arthritis patients showed that after 6 months of IFS (focusing on pain, getting curious about it, asking parts questions), there were significant improvements in physical manifestation of arthritis -- some went into complete remission
- A session with a woman who had 17 years of debilitating back pain: after one session where she listened to the angry, fearful, and pleasing parts stuck in a car accident scene, the back pain disappeared and hadn't returned a year later
- The key was that she continued working with her parts daily after the session

The message: when you refuse to listen, you can turn your parts into inner terrorists who will use your body to get your attention. The medical system too often kills the messenger rather than helping you get the message.

## Daily Practice
Whenever a sensation or symptom comes up, pay attention to it. What message is it trying to send?
