# IFS Worksheets - When to Use Each

These 10 worksheets form a practical toolkit that roughly follows a progression from early discovery through deeper healing. You don't need to go in order -- reach for whichever one fits the moment.

## 01 - Parts Map
**Phase:** Early / getting started
**Use when:** You want a bird's-eye view of your internal landscape. Good starting point for any new exploration.
**What it does:** Helps you name parts, locate them in your body, identify their roles, what they want for you, and what they fear would happen if they stopped. Space for 3+ parts. Work in 2-5 minute blocks; pause if distress rises above 6/10.

## 02 - Parts Profile Card
**Phase:** Early to mid
**Use when:** You can name a part and want to understand it more deeply -- its triggers, thoughts, feelings, body sensations, behaviors, and what it needs from you.
**What it does:** A deep-dive portrait of a single part. Think of it as the character sheet for one part after the map gives you the cast.

## 03 - Self Energy Check-In
**Phase:** Any session, any time
**Use when:** You want to gauge how much Self you have access to right now. Great before doing any parts work, or just as a daily pulse check.
**What it does:** Rate each of the 8 Cs (Calm, Clarity, Curiosity, Compassion, Confidence, Courage, Creativity, Connectedness) on 0-10. Note what presence feels like today and one small step to increase it.

## 04 - Unblending Steps
**Phase:** When a part has taken over
**Use when:** You're activated -- a part is running the show and you've lost the separation between Self and part. You feel consumed by an emotion or pattern.
**What it does:** Walks you through naming the blended part, rating distress (0-10), asking the part for space, tracking what helped create distance, and setting a cue for next time. Safety protocol: if distress stays above 6/10, stop and ground (feet on floor, name 5 things you see).

## 05 - Six Fs Interview
**Phase:** Mid-phase
**Use when:** You've identified a protector and want a structured way to build relationship with it. The 6 Fs are the core IFS protocol for getting to know a part.
**What it does:** Walks through Find (locate it), Focus (notice details), Flesh out (age, appearance, beliefs), Feel toward it (check for Self energy), beFriend (what does it need), and check Fears (what it's afraid would happen if its needs were met).

## 06 - Polarization Map
**Phase:** When two parts are clashing
**Use when:** You feel torn, stuck in internal conflict, or like two forces are fighting inside you (e.g., a part that wants to push hard vs. a part that wants to rest).
**What it does:** Names both parts and their stances, surfaces each one's positive intent, then finds the shared underlying goal. Maps the cost of the fight and proposes one small experiment to reduce intensity.

## 07 - Protectors Consent Sheet
**Phase:** Before any exile work
**Use when:** You're considering going toward deeper pain or exile content. CRITICAL: never move toward exile material without protector permission.
**What it does:** Formalizes the consent process -- what the protector fears, minimum safety conditions, warning signs to stop, what it wants you to promise, and a tiny trial step (1-3 min max with exit plan). Includes a post-trial check-in.

## 08 - Exile Witnessing Journal
**Phase:** Trauma-sensitive work ONLY
**Use when:** Protectors have given consent, you have stable Self access, and ideally clinical oversight. This is the deepest worksheet.
**What it does:** Brief, titrated witnessing -- Self check-in before/after (0-10), the exile's age or scene headline (no graphic detail), what the exile wants you to know (1-2 sentences), how Self responded. Strict time limit (1-3 min). Emergency protocol: stop immediately if distress exceeds 6/10.

## 09 - Trigger and Protector Cycle
**Phase:** When you notice reactive patterns
**Use when:** You keep getting activated by the same kinds of situations and want to see the chain clearly -- trigger to protector to behavior to cost.
**What it does:** Maps the full cycle: trigger cue, which protectors activate, strategies used (avoid/numb/fix/attack/perform), short-term relief vs. longer-term cost, the exile being protected (if known), alternative micro-choices to test, and results of experiments.

## 10 - Unburdening Plan and Aftercare
**Phase:** After a part releases a burden
**Use when:** You've done unburdening work (with a therapist or deep self-led practice) and a part has let go of something it was carrying. The part now needs a new job.
**What it does:** Names the burden released, defines a new role for the part, sets daily practices to reinforce the new role, describes how Self will relate to this part now, identifies early warning signs the burden is returning, and creates a follow-up plan.

---

**General progression:** Map (01) -> Profile (02) -> Self check (03) -> Unblend if needed (04) -> Interview protectors (05) -> Map polarizations (06) -> Get protector consent (07) -> Witness exiles carefully (08) -> Track trigger cycles (09) -> Consolidate after unburdening (10)

But in practice, 03 (Self Energy Check-In) and 04 (Unblending) are tools you'll reach for constantly, and 09 (Trigger Cycle) is great for pattern recognition at any stage.
