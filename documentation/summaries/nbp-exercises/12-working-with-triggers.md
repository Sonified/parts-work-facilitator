# Exercise 12: Working with Triggers
**Chapter:** 9 (Life Lessons and Tor-Mentors)
**Phase:** When previous exercises have activated parts
**Time:** 5-10 minutes

## Purpose
A short grounding practice for when inner work has stirred things up. This is the safety net exercise.

## Full Exercise

Notice what's happening in your body and your mind after having gone into that inner world for a few minutes. If there are any parts triggered by that, rather than blending with them, notice them.

As you do so, ask them to separate from you just a little so you can be with them without being them.

If it's possible to be curious about their triggers from that more separated state, just ask why this was so hard for them. What do they want you to know?

As you're with them and not in them, see if you can reassure them that you're still there. Remind them that you're not young and that you can help them too. You understand that this is hard work and it's scary for some of your parts, but you're there with them.

As you're with these parts in this compassionate way, remind them that you've been taking care of them and yourself for a long time. You have some wisdom about how to help everybody feel better and you're going to act on that wisdom.

When the time feels right, do whatever helps you shift focus back outside.

## Key Insight
You are a person who has courage, confidence, clarity, feels connected, and is grounded. If you're feeling anything that tells you you're not, those messages are coming from parts who don't know who you are. They often believe you're much younger than you are. Don't totally blend with them and enter their world -- instead, reassure them, separate from them, and help them trust that you can do this because you're not a little kid anymore.
