# Example Parts from IFS Literature

What parts actually look and sound like in practice. Use this for reference when identifying your own parts -- not as a checklist, but as a sense of the range and granularity.

---

## Exiles

Before they got hurt, these were the playful, creative, trusting, innocent, open parts. After trauma or attachment injury, they absorb extreme beliefs and emotions (burdens) and get locked away.

**What they commonly carry:**
- "I am worthless" / "No one loves me" / "I am unlovable"
- Deep shame, fear, helplessness, despair, emptiness, sorrow
- Loneliness, abandonment, confusion, fragility
- Frozen trauma memories and the emotions from those scenes

**What they long for:** To come out of hiding, be healed, valued, loved, redeemed

**What gets exiled with them:** Their curiosity, spontaneity, courage, connectedness, capacity for joy, awe, and love

**Key behaviors when they break out:** Overwhelming floods of emotion, regression to childhood states, can't get out of bed, feeling like you might die, desperate neediness

**Examples from the book:**
- A part that feels totally bereft, lonely, empty, and worthless -- triggered by an inner critic's attack
- Parts frozen in abuse scenes, believing the person is still a child in danger
- Parts carrying the belief that the world is very dangerous and it's best to stay invisible
- Exiles whose sense of worthlessiness unconsciously steers toward spiritualities or gurus that promise redemption
- The delightful inner children who got hurt and then abandoned -- we lose access to intense joy, awe, and love when they're exiled

---

## Managers (Preemptive Protectors)

Work constantly to prevent exiles from getting triggered. They manage relationships, appearance, and performance. They are parentified inner children -- young themselves, tired, stressed, never off duty.

**Common manager parts:**

| Part | What it does | What it's really trying to do |
|------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| Inner Critic / Drill Sergeant | Attacks you mercilessly for mistakes, shames you | Motivate you to try harder so you won't get hurt; yells at you the way parents/teachers once did |
| Perfectionist | Demands flawless performance | Keep you safe by making sure no one can criticize you |
| People Pleaser / Caretaker | Takes care of everyone else, neglects self | Control relationships so no one gets upset or leaves |
| Hypervigilant Part | Constantly scanning for danger | Prevent any triggering situation from occurring |
| Intellectual / Analyzer | Keeps you in your head, out of your body | If you don't feel, you don't get triggered |
| Striver / Achiever | Drives relentless productivity and ambition | Earn worth through accomplishment; stay too busy to feel |
| Self-Hating Part / Saboteur | Tears you down when you start feeling good | Keep you small and below the radar where it's safe |
| Numbing Part | Dampens body sensation and emotion | Reduce feeling so exiles can't break through |
| Controller | Rigid rules, micromanages behavior | Maintain predictable, safe environment |
| Belonging/Conforming Part | Goes through the motions of what's expected | Fit in, don't stand out, don't get rejected |

**What they carry:** Responsibility, guilt, anger, worry, anxiety, fatigue

**Key quality:** They feel tired and old. They are working all the time -- some never sleep.

**What they become after unburdening:** Inner critics → cheerleaders or sage advisors. Caretakers → help set boundaries. Hypervigilant parts → advisors on who is safe.

## Firefighters (Reactive Protectors)

Activate after an exile has been triggered. Desperate, impulsive, little regard for collateral damage. Their job is to put out the fire right now -- douse the flames, get you higher than them, or distract until it burns out.

**Common firefighter parts:**

| Part | What it does | What it's really trying to do |
|------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| Binge Part | Eating, spending, consuming to excess | Take you out of your body, become an unfeeling machine |
| Substance-Seeking Part | Alcohol, drugs, prescriptions | Get higher than the flames of exiled emotion |
| Sexual/Pornography Part | Compulsive sexual behavior, pornography | Seek intensity/connection/comfort to override exiled pain |
| Rage Part | Explosive anger, verbal or physical | Force the world to back off; discharge unbearable energy |
| Dissociative Part | Spacing out, going foggy, falling asleep | Leave the scene entirely when it's too much |
| Self-Harm Part | Cutting, physical pain | Get out of emotional body; control something; express what can't be said |
| Suicidal Part | Thoughts or impulses of ending life | The ultimate escape when no other firefighter solution works |
| Seeker / Recruiter | Moves from one guru/practice/person to another | Find the one thing that will permanently make the exiles feel better |
| Spiritual Bypass Part | Addicted to meditation or spiritual practice | Transcend the exiles; feel good without healing |
| Entertainment/Distraction Part | Netflix, social media, games, doomscrolling | Numb the pain, keep attention anywhere but on the exiles |
| Rebel / Defiant Part | Breaks rules, acts out, says "screw it" | Refuse to be controlled; reclaim autonomy from managers |

**What they carry:** Stress, anger, shame, guilt, fatigue

**Key quality:** Desperate. Will escalate with little regard for consequences. Like babysitters who can't get the kids to stop screaming.

**What they become after unburdening:** The addict part wants to help you connect with people. The rageful part wants to help with discerning who is safe. The cutting part wanted to protect from abuse and control overwhelming rage.

## How Parts Interact

Parts don't exist in isolation. They form systems:

- **Critic → Worthlessness → Binge cycle:** Critic attacks → exile feels worthless → firefighter binges to escape → critic attacks for the binge → worthlessness deepens → cycle repeats for days
- **Manager-Firefighter escalation:** The harder managers try to control firefighters, the stronger firefighters become -- can escalate dangerously
- **Exile breakout → Firefighter scramble:** When an exile gets triggered and breaks through containment, firefighters immediately scramble to douse the fire
- **Critic overridden → Next manager activates:** If the critic gets overridden, another manager kicks in -- maybe dissociation or sleep

## Important Reminders

- These categories describe **roles parts were forced into**, not the essence of the parts themselves
- Every part, even the most extreme, was once in a valuable natural state and can return to it
- Parts are frozen in time -- they often don't know you're an adult now
- A part's age, name, and appearance come from the part itself, not from labeling its behavior
- The same part can shift roles (a manager can become a firefighter under enough pressure)
- Don't mistake the burden for the part that carries it
